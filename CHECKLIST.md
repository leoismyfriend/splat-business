# 📱 Field Scanning Checklist

Use this before, during, and after every scan job.

---

## Before You Arrive

- [ ] Charge phone to 100%
- [ ] Clear at least 4 GB of free storage
- [ ] Download / update Luma AI app
- [ ] Confirm appointment + access with client
- [ ] Pack: phone, charger, small tripod (optional)

---

## On Arrival — Prep the Space

- [ ] Open every curtain and blind (natural light = better result)
- [ ] Turn on **every** light in the property
- [ ] Remove people, pets, and moving objects from rooms
- [ ] Close toilet lids, tidy counters (it's permanent)
- [ ] Note any mirrors or glass surfaces (can confuse the AI — scan slowly around them)

---

## Recording — Room by Room

### Setup
- [ ] Open Luma AI → **Capture** → **3D Scene**
- [ ] Hold phone at chest height, camera pointed slightly downward
- [ ] Move at **half your normal walking speed**

### Per Room
- [ ] Walk the full perimeter close to walls
- [ ] Sweep floor-to-ceiling in each corner
- [ ] Overlap passes — revisit each area from 2–3 angles
- [ ] Spend extra time on complex geometry (stairs, archways, built-ins)
- [ ] 4–8 minutes per room is normal

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ Moving too fast — blurry splats, missing geometry
- ❌ Skipping corners — holes in the model
- ❌ Scanning at night without full artificial lighting
- ❌ Filming through glass without slowing down

---

## After Recording

- [ ] Review the preview in Luma before leaving the property
- [ ] If gaps or blur are visible → re-scan that section immediately
- [ ] Upload to Luma (auto-processes in 30–60 min)
- [ ] Wait for the completion email

---

## Delivery

- [ ] Open the Luma scene → Share → **Embed**
- [ ] Copy the `<iframe>` code
- [ ] Drop into `client-page-template/index.html` (update property name, contact info)
- [ ] Test on desktop AND mobile before sending
- [ ] Send client the live URL or the HTML file

---

## Pro Tips

**Mirrors:** Approach slowly, circle wide. Don't point camera directly at mirror for more than 1–2 seconds.

**Staircases:** Walk up and down twice, once facing each direction.

**Outdoor spaces:** Works great in patios/gardens. Avoid direct sunlight into lens.

**Large rooms:** Do a slow figure-8 path through the center, then hug the walls.
